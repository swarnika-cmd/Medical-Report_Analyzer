=== COMMON MEDICAL REFERENCE RANGES ===

These are standard adult reference ranges. Values may vary slightly between laboratories.
Always refer to the specific lab's reference range printed on the report.

--- COMPLETE BLOOD COUNT (CBC) ---
Hemoglobin (Hb): Male 13.5-17.5 g/dL, Female 12.0-16.0 g/dL
Red Blood Cells (RBC): Male 4.5-5.5 million/mcL, Female 4.0-5.0 million/mcL
White Blood Cells (WBC): 4,000-11,000 cells/mcL
Platelets: 150,000-400,000 cells/mcL
Hematocrit (HCT): Male 38.3-48.6%, Female 35.5-44.9%
Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV): 80-100 fL
Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH): 27-33 pg
MCHC: 32-36 g/dL
RDW: 11.5-14.5%
ESR: Male 0-15 mm/hr, Female 0-20 mm/hr

--- WBC DIFFERENTIAL ---
Neutrophils: 40-70%
Lymphocytes: 20-40%
Monocytes: 2-8%
Eosinophils: 1-4%
Basophils: 0-1%

--- LIPID PANEL ---
Total Cholesterol: Desirable <200 mg/dL, Borderline 200-239, High >=240
LDL Cholesterol: Optimal <100, Near Optimal 100-129, Borderline 130-159, High >=160
HDL Cholesterol: Low <40 (risk factor), Good 40-59, Excellent >=60
Triglycerides: Normal <150, Borderline 150-199, High 200-499, Very High >=500
VLDL: 5-40 mg/dL

--- BLOOD SUGAR ---
Fasting Blood Sugar: Normal 70-100 mg/dL, Prediabetes 100-125, Diabetes >=126
Post-Prandial (2 hr): Normal <140, Prediabetes 140-199, Diabetes >=200
HbA1c: Normal <5.7%, Prediabetes 5.7-6.4%, Diabetes >=6.5%
Random Blood Sugar: Normal <140 mg/dL

--- LIVER FUNCTION TESTS (LFT) ---
ALT (SGPT): 7-56 U/L
AST (SGOT): 10-40 U/L
ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase): 44-147 U/L
Total Bilirubin: 0.1-1.2 mg/dL
Direct Bilirubin: 0-0.3 mg/dL
Indirect Bilirubin: 0.1-0.9 mg/dL
Total Protein: 6.0-8.3 g/dL
Albumin: 3.5-5.5 g/dL
Globulin: 2.0-3.5 g/dL
A/G Ratio: 1.0-2.5
GGT (Gamma GT): Male 8-61 U/L, Female 5-36 U/L

--- KIDNEY FUNCTION TESTS (KFT/RFT) ---
Blood Urea: 7-20 mg/dL
BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen): 7-20 mg/dL
Creatinine: Male 0.7-1.3 mg/dL, Female 0.6-1.1 mg/dL
Uric Acid: Male 3.4-7.0 mg/dL, Female 2.4-6.0 mg/dL
eGFR: Normal >90 mL/min, Mild decrease 60-89, Moderate 30-59, Severe 15-29

--- THYROID FUNCTION ---
TSH: 0.4-4.0 mIU/L
Free T3: 2.3-4.2 pg/mL
Free T4: 0.8-1.8 ng/dL
Total T3: 80-200 ng/dL
Total T4: 5.1-14.1 mcg/dL

--- VITAMINS & MINERALS ---
Vitamin D (25-OH): Deficient <20 ng/mL, Insufficient 20-29, Sufficient 30-100
Vitamin B12: 200-900 pg/mL (deficient <200)
Iron: Male 65-175 mcg/dL, Female 50-170 mcg/dL
Ferritin: Male 20-500 ng/mL, Female 20-200 ng/mL
Calcium: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
Magnesium: 1.7-2.2 mg/dL
Phosphorus: 2.5-4.5 mg/dL
Sodium: 136-145 mEq/L
Potassium: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
Chloride: 98-106 mEq/L
Folic Acid: 2.7-17.0 ng/mL

--- INFLAMMATION MARKERS ---
CRP (C-Reactive Protein): <1.0 mg/L (low risk), 1-3 (moderate), >3 (high risk)
hs-CRP: <1.0 mg/L (low risk)

--- DIABETES MARKERS ---
Fasting Insulin: 2.6-24.9 mcIU/mL
HOMA-IR: <2.0 (normal), >2.5 (insulin resistance likely)

--- COMMON DEFICIENCY INDICATORS ---
Low Hemoglobin + Low MCV + Low Iron = Iron deficiency anemia (eat: spinach, red meat, lentils, beans)
Low Vitamin D = Bone health risk (eat: fatty fish, eggs, fortified milk; get sunlight)
Low Vitamin B12 = Nerve/energy issues (eat: meat, eggs, dairy, fortified cereals)
Low Calcium = Bone health risk (eat: dairy, leafy greens, almonds)
High LDL + High Total Cholesterol = Heart disease risk (reduce: fried food, red meat; increase: oats, nuts, olive oil)
High Blood Sugar + High HbA1c = Diabetes risk (reduce: sugar, refined carbs; increase: fiber, vegetables)
High Uric Acid = Gout risk (reduce: red meat, organ meats, alcohol; increase: water, cherries)
High Creatinine = Kidney stress (reduce: protein overload, NSAIDs; increase: water intake)
